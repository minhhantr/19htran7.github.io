Raising Pikachu

Raise the pikachu! By clicking on the pikachu, you get points in which you
can spend in the shop. Take care of the pikachu by feeding it when it is hungry
and buying it toys when it gets bored. If you get enough points, you can also
buy partners or TMs which improves your currency income. Once you get enough points
to buy the thunderstone, you evolve your pikachu and win the game!

Describe some programming concepts or structures you learned
in this unit that you applied in your game.

Some programming concepts or structures I learned in this unit that I applied
in my games are event-handling, sequences and iretation. I used event-handling as
a way to keep the story of the game going; for example, if a player wins, the Pikachu
evolves to Raichu, then I will name the event "Win" and I use event-handling to change
costumes and backdrops. About iretation and sequence, I used them to create the costume
of the Pikachu; I changed the Pikachu's costumes and music in the sequence that I wanted,
then I used iretation for these sequential codes to make the Pikachu look like it's moving

Did you encounter any problems or struggles while working on this project?
If you did, how did you solve or overcome them?

I did encounter struggle while working on this project. The struggle was the scoring
system; my partner and I didn't know how to make the hundreds or thousands of the scoring
system work. we overcame our struggle by asking for help from Ms.Terry Nguyen. However,
when we got help, we didn't simply just listen to how Ms.Terry conducted us to write codes
and copy them, we always tried to understand the concepts before we wrote the codes down

What aspects of your game do you like best?

The aspects of my game the I personally like best are the requirement of players' patience
and their deciding skill. Players have to decide when is the right time to buy items and which
item they should buy in order to gain currency and to win quicklier. Beside that, players have
to be patient because they have to click on the Pikachu constantly to earn currency which is the
only way to win this game.

If more time was given for you to work on your game, what would you improve
or modify about your game?

If I had more time to work on my game, I would modify the amount of currency earned, or fix
the prices of items. That is because of the approximate amount of time that players take to win
this game is about 7 to 8 minutes. I think the best amount of time to win a game is about 4 to 5
minutes. In addition, I want to make my game's shop look better by separating TMs and partners to
another type of shops called upgrade.
